About the Author

Dr. Allen E. Goldenthal is an author, scientist, and global thinker whose work bridges the worlds of fiction and reality. With a doctorate in biotechnology and decades of experience in scientific and public health leadership, Dr. Goldenthal has published extensively in academic and professional settings. His transition into fiction and narrative nonfiction reflects a deep passion for exploring the moral, philosophical, and spiritual questions of our time. Whether unraveling the mysteries of ancient genealogies, charting speculative futures, or delving into prophetic symbolism, his books are marked by rigorous research, vivid imagination, and a profound empathy for the human condition.

Dr. Allen E. Goldenthal works and writes from his homes in New Zealand and China. As an internationally known consultant and auditor of the biopharmaceutical industry, his work has taken him across the globe, but no matter where he may be, New Zealand is always home. He is the author of the Kahana Chronicles series, uncovering the hidden influence of a family who shaped history from the shadows. This series is more than fiction—it's a personal journey, as Dr. Goldenthal is a descendant of the Kahana.

Allen Goldenthal initially trained as a veterinarian, though unlike James Herriot, never felt compelled to write about animals. Born in Canada, he sought warmer climates and found his paradise in New Zealand. His passion for research led him through academic posts at Massey University and the University of Macau, executive leadership in biopharma, and now, global auditing for drug safety. He divides time between his farm in Whangarei, New Zealand and his home in Panzhihua, China. His genealogical investigations into the Kahana family—figures long hidden by history—serve as the foundation for his Kahana Chronicles series.

America's War Against the Jews

America’s War Against the Jews explores the persistent, evolving nature of antisemitism within the United States. From early colonial prejudice to today’s politically charged rhetoric, Dr. Goldenthal traces how anti-Jewish hatred has adapted to shifting societal norms. While criticism of Israeli policy may be legitimate, it often morphs into calls for the annihilation of the Jewish state—something no other ethnicity faces on the world stage. In doing so, anti-Zionism reveals itself not as a political stance, but as a modern mask for ancient hatred.

Through historical analysis, cultural critique, and recorded documents, the book follows the transformation of antisemitism over generations: from religious blame to accusations of elitism, from scapegoating to envy of success. The Jewish people have been condemned for remaining apart—then equally condemned for integrating. Now, their longing for a homeland is maligned as oppression. This book lays bare the contradictions, and asks a difficult question: has America truly been a safe haven, or just a more polite battlefield? The answers in the book may even surprise those Jews living in American that have never bothered to search for the truth.
